Best way: make feedback easy, specific, and low-friction. Don’t just ask “what do you think?” Ask people to test one thing and answer a few questions.

For your paycheck/timecard apps, I’d do this:

1. Add a feedback button inside the app

Put a visible button like:

“Send feedback / report bad result”

Have it open a simple Google Form, GitHub issue page, or email link.

Ask:

What were you trying to do?
Did the app give the result you expected?
Was anything confusing?
Did the math look right?
What feature would make this more useful?
Would you use this again?
Optional: email if you want a reply.

For a payroll app, also add:

Do not upload real sensitive pay documents unless you are comfortable sharing them. Remove SSN, address, employee ID, and employer details first.

2. Post in places where workers and builders hang out

Good places for your type of app:

r/sideprojects
r/webdev
r/learnprogramming
r/SaaS
r/EntrepreneurRideAlong
r/Payroll
r/antiwork, but carefully — ask for usefulness, not promotion
Facebook groups for warehouse workers, factory workers, payroll help, or labor rights
Discord servers for indie hackers or beginner devs

Your strongest angle is:

“I built this because my own paycheck was wrong and I wanted a way to double-check hours vs pay.”

That is way more relatable than “check out my app.”

3. Give people a task, not just a link

Instead of saying:

Please give feedback on my app.

Say:

Can you try uploading a sample paystub or fake test PDF and tell me whether the results are understandable? I’m especially looking for feedback on the report screen, math trustworthiness, and whether this feels safe enough to use.

People are more likely to respond when you tell them exactly what feedback you need.

4. Make a fake demo file

Since your app deals with paystubs/timecards, many people won’t upload real documents. You need:

sample timecard PDF
sample paystub PDF
example output report
screenshots of the workflow

Then people can test without risking personal info.

5. Track feedback in a simple spreadsheet

Use columns like:

Date	Source	Feedback	Bug/Feature/Confusion	Priority	Fixed?

This keeps you from getting overwhelmed.

6. Ask 3 feedback questions everywhere

Use these three:

What part was confusing?
Would you trust this to check your paycheck? Why or why not?
What would make this useful enough to come back to?

For your app, question #2 is the most important. Your biggest problem is probably not features — it is trust.

7. Post a simple feedback message

You can use this:

I built a small app after having repeated issues with my paycheck not matching my hours. The goal is to help workers compare timecards and paystubs and spot possible errors before they become a bigger problem.

I’m looking for honest feedback, especially on whether the app is understandable, trustworthy, and useful. You can test it with fake/sample PDFs — please don’t upload sensitive personal documents unless you’re comfortable doing so.

Main questions:

Is the result/report easy to understand?
Would you trust this kind of tool to double-check pay?
What feature would make it more useful?

App: [your link]
Feedback form: [your form link]

8. What feedback matters most right now

For your stage, focus on:

Did users understand what the app does?
Did they know what file to upload?
Did the output make sense?
Did anything break?
Did they trust the math?
Would they use it after getting paid?
What made them nervous?

Don’t worry too much about monetization feedback yet. First prove people actually want to check their pay this way.
